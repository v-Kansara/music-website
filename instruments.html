Chordophone Instruments

Chordophones are stringed instruments, such as the violin, sitar, veena, etc. They are played by plucking, striking, bowing or other ways that vibrate the tensioned string.

Membranophone Instruments

Membranophones are instruments with tensioned membranes spanned across their surface, such as the tabla, mrudangam, etc. They are played by striking, rubbing, knocking or other ways that vibrate the tensioned membrane on the instrument.

Aerophone Instruments

Aerophones are usually hollow instruments, such as the flute. They are played by blowing, whistling, or other ways that vibrate the air inside of the instrument.

Idiophone Instruments

Idiophones are solid instruments, such as the manjira, bells, etc. They are played by striking, banging, ringing or other ways that vibrate the whole body of the instrument.
